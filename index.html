Watch Your Speech: Text-aware Video-to-Speech Synthesis with Lip-Reading Guidance

Anonymous submission

Abstract

Video-to-speech synthesis aims to generate natural-sounding speech from silent talking-face videos while ensuring the accurate content of the spoken sentences. Although significant progress has been made, concurrently achieving high sentence-level accuracy and excellent speech quality remains a major challenge. This difficulty stems from the inherent one-to-many mapping problem, where a single silent video can correspond to multiple valid phonetic expressions. To address this, this paper proposes a novel text-aware video-to-speech synthesis framework that leverages lip-reading guidance to convert the task into a well-defined one-to-one mapping. The proposed framework features an embedding fusion module that effectively preserves textual context and synchronizes it with video sequences during speech synthesis. Evaluations on the large-scale LRS2 and LRS3 datasets demonstrate that the proposed method significantly outperforms existing approaches. Specifically, the model achieves a new state-of-the-art in textual accuracy with a substantial reduction in word error rate, demonstrates highly competitive performance on other quantitative metrics, and receives superior scores in human-evaluated mean opinion scores for speech quality.
